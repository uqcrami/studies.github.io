Dear Participant,

You are invited to participate in a Study of Expressivity in Simple Artificial Forms, conducted by Carlos Ramirez, a postgraduate student from The University of Queensland. Before you decide to take part, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take your time to read the following information carefully. Feel free to ask any questions if you need clarification.

Purpose of the Study

The objective of this study is to investigate how basic agents can evoke emotional responses from human observers. We aim to employ a novel analytical approach to examine the results, with the goal of identifying key expressive characteristics. This analysis will contribute to refining our understanding of expressivity in artificial agents. Additionally, we seek to determine how expressivity is manifested within the constraints of limited agent capabilities.

Study Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to watch fifteen videos, lasting no longer than 30 seconds each, then describe what you see, and respond to a number of questions such as "What in the forms actions mostly resulted in your behaviour prediction?". Your participation will be paid at a rate of 9GBP and should not extend past 20 minutes.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation in this study is ENTIRELY VOLUNTARY. You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without providing any reason. Remuneration will be paid only for completed studies.

Confidentiality

Any information collected during the study will be kept confidential and will only be accessible to the research team. Your responses will be anonymized, and no identifying information will be associated with your data.

Data Storage and Use

The data collected during the study will be securely stored at The University of Queensland Research Data Management Centre, adhering to the highest standards of excellence in research data management as outlined by the University of Queensland's ethics on data. This data will be used solely for research purposes. Any findings from the study may be published in scientific journals or presented at conferences, while ensuring that your identity remains anonymous throughout the process.
